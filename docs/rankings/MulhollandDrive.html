Mulholland Drive

2001

Mulholland Drive was my favorite Lynch film for a long time. It first changed my life on March 12, 2018. I was 17 and I had only heard it was a lesbian romance that revels in the darkness of Hollywood. Yes, it is that, yet so much more. It's narrative is a little linear, but the midpoint of the movie absolutely switches gears and the audience is left to interpret what the film's dual nature means. I love that the movie doesn't offer answers. There are tiny old people, and a magical homeless women who controls dreams, and a studio exec who is really particular about espresso. It's weird like most of Lynch's work, but in a way that aches with love and passion. Naomi Watts and Laura Elena Harring have magical energy that subsumes the viewer. I had the opportunity to watch this in theaters last year for the first time, and it was like seeing it for the first time again. I was having a bad day that day, and I left the theatre with a renewed love of people, the world, and art. I hope it can have the same effect on you.
