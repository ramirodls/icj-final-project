Inland Empire

2006

Inland Empire is not only my favorite Lynch movie, but it comfortably resides within my top 5 movies of all time (though Mulholland Drive doesn't lag that far behind either). This is Lynch's most recent film, and it's unclear whether he'll make another one, but if this is his last, it is surely his magnum opus. To be honest, this isn't the easiest watch. It's three hours long, and once the movie really gets going, it starts opening new layers into the narrative that leave you wondering what's happening. As you can imagine, I'm generally against 'understanding' movies. Inland Empire is an experience that I think has a lot to say under its complex surface. Like some of Lynch's other films, it's about womanhood and Hollywood. Hollywood is a machine that eats up Laura Dern, splits her identity and sense of self in every which way, and then spits her out broken. It's brutal. There is violence, and overly long shots of people walking, and weird graphics, and talking rabbits, but once the credits roll, I feel like I've seen a truly great piece of art. I watched it three times in one week during a theatrical run at Austin Film Society. 9 hours of my life were spent watching this movie, and I would never give them back. You truly have to see it to believe it.
